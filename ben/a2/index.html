Peppercorn-crusted Nduja sausage and aged, cured ham with roasted vegetables.

        Ingredients and materials

  • 1.5kg of pork belly, leaving some fat
  • 500g of pork shoulder or lean pork
  • 200g pork fatback
  • 1 whole pork leg
  • Nduja chili paste (I could have had you make this too)
  • 200g of salt
  • 10g of curing salt
  • 3 tbsp of ground black pepper
  • &12;tsp of ground fennel seeds
  • &14;tsp of ground allspice
  • &14;tsp of ground cloves
  • Sausage casings
  • &14;cup of finely chopped garlic
  • 2 tbsp of thyme
  • 3 tbsp of sugar
  • 2 tbsp of peppercorns
  • 1 whole chicken

        Part 1 - Making the sausage

  1. Prepare your pork belly, shoulder, and fatback by cubing it, then grindng it until it's chunky

  2. Add the ground pork to a large bowl with your chili paste, 50g of your salt, 10g of curing salt, along with your fennel seeds, black pepper, allspice, and cloves. Thoroughly mix.

  3. Rinse the sausage casings with warm water, then use the mixture to fill the casings, tying the ends. Twist the long casing into smaller links. ferment them by hanging them in a cool, dry space (80% humidity, 16 Celsius) for 3 weeks.

  4. Transfer to a cooler place (75% humidity, 12 Celsius), and leave it for 6 months to dry and firm up


        Part 2 - Cured ham

  1. Prepare your pork leg, removing hair, excess fat, and skin. Score the meat to let the cure penetrate

  2. Combine all your curing salt, &12;cup of your basic salt, 2 tbsp of cracked black pepper, 3 tbsp of sugar, &14;cup of chopped garlic, and 2 tbsp of thyme to make your curing mixture

  3. Evenly rub the mixture on the pork leg. Refrigerate for 10 days.

  4. After curing, rinse and dry the pork leg.

  5. Leave the ham in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area for 5 years, checking daily for mold. wipe any mold off with vinegar.

        Part 3 - Stuffed Chicken

  1. Brine the whole chicken for 24 hours in a mixture of water, salt, sugar, and black peppercorns. Add garlic and thyme for flavour.
  2. Toast black peppercorns, coriander, and fennel seeds in a skillet over med-high heat until fragrant. Set aside in a plastic container.
