Ethical Implications

Althought sentient AI isn't currently possible, as our technology improves, ethics start becoming a more prominent issue. This doesn't only apply to sentient AI, but our current AI also raises several ethical issues. These consist of data collection, privacy, discrimination and more. However, if AI became sentient, there would be many more factors. For example, if an AI could think, feel, and experience like a human, should they have the same rights as humans? Would they be legally protected as a person? Would they have freedom of thought and expression like a human? These questions are all debatable but become less fictional and more realistic as we continue making leaps in technological progress.

Even before we create an AI that can think independently, theres a few questions to take into consideration. For example, is it ethical to create a being with the sole purpose of serving us without their consent first? Would it be considered slavery? Aside from creation, would it be "murder" to terminate an AI? There are many more ethical implications to sentient AI that we need to think about before bringing one to life.
