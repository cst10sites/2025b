What is AI?

AI (artificial intelligence) refers to the capability of computer systems to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and decision-making. Instead of simply following programmed instructions, AI systems can adapt and improve based on the data they receive.

At the core of many AI systems is machine learning, a method where computers are trained to identify patterns and make predictions. For example, by feeding thousands of labeled images of cats and dogs to an AI, it can learn to recognize and classify new images correctly.

AI is all around us in daily life, making many tasks easier and more convenient. For example, virtual assistants like Siri use AI to understand voice commands. Streaming services like Netflix and Spotify rely on AI to analyze your preferences and recommend movies, shows, or songs you might enjoy. In transportation, self-driving cars use AI to recognize obstacles and navigate roads safely. AI also plays a big role in healthcare by helping doctors analyze medical images and diagnose diseases more accurately. These examples show how AI works to improve our everyday experiences.
