Meet the Winners

Over the years, many talented clowns have graced the Clown Olympics stage. Here are some of the greatest champions in the competition's history:

  • Emmett Kelly – Famous for his "Weary Willie" character, a sad-faced hobo clown, symbolizing the essence of sadness and comedy.
  • Red Skelton – A comedic legend who performed as "Freddy the Freeloader," a bumbling, loveable character.
  • Charlie Chaplin – Known for his "Tramp" persona in silent films, Chaplin's physical comedy made him a clowning icon.
  • Bozo the Clown – A household name in clowning, Bozo became a TV legend with his red nose, colorful hair, and zany antics.
  • David Larible – An extraordinary clown performer known for his work with Ringling Bros. and Barnum & Bailey Circus.
  • Joseph Grimaldi – The "father" of the modern clown, known for his white face makeup and iconic "Harlequin" persona.
  • Pennywise the Clown – Though fictional, Pennywise remains a terrifying yet fascinating figure in clown culture, made famous by Stephen King's novel *It*.
  • Mikhail Shchepkin – Renowned Russian clown who became famous for his performances in the Soviet Union's circus scene.
  • Slava Polunin – A legendary Russian clown known for his performances in Cirque du Soleil and his own theatrical productions.
  • Gagman and Gagwoman – Popular comedic duo from the 1920s, known for their slapstick humor and clever skits.

Other Notable Clowns

Besides the winners of the Clown Olympics, many other clowns have left a huge mark in the world of performance. Here are a few who had groundbreaking performances or made a big impact during their careers:

  • Lou Jacobs – A famous circus clown known for his "ringmaster" style. He was one of the first to perform with the famous "clown car," fitting dozens of clowns into a tiny car.
  • Bill Irwin – A pioneer in physical comedy, Irwin performed both in traditional circus clowning and modern theater, earning an Emmy Award for his unique performances.
  • Joey the Clown – Joey became a sensation for his heartwarming and humorous acts, often performing in hospitals, making him a beloved figure in the "clown care" movement.
  • Cirque du Soleil's Clowns – The clowns of Cirque du Soleil have become iconic figures in the world of modern circus performance, mixing theater, acrobatics, and clowning in innovative ways.
  • Patch Adams – While not a traditional clown, Patch Adams revolutionized the concept of therapeutic clowning by working with children in hospitals, using humor to promote healing.
  • Jango Edwards – A clown who became internationally famous for his "clown theater" style, blending satire and slapstick to entertain both children and adults in European circuses.
  • David Shiner – Famous for his collaboration with Cirque du Soleil, Shiner's clown performances mix physical comedy with sharp timing and acrobatics.
  • Sammy the Clown – Known for his famous “nose-sliding” act, which became a viral sensation during a live television performance in 1987.
  • Fool Moon – A highly skilled mime artist whose performance in New York's iconic "Clown Week" made him a sensation for combining traditional mime with modern clowning techniques.

Current Champion

David Larible is considered one of the best living clowns, having won multiple awards and accolades for his incredible circus performances.
