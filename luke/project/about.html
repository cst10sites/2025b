About the Clown Olympics

The Clown Olympics is a creatorial-hypothetical event that ponders what would occur if Olympic-type competition met the hilarious and colorful lateral of clowning. It celebrates the art of clowning in a playful manner while joy, humor, and physical comedy are brought center stage.

While the Clown Olympics itself is not a bona fide event, it draws inspiration from several festivals and clown traditions around the world, such as Mexico's Festival Internacional del Payaso, and the performances of world-famous clowns like David Larible and Slava Polunin. These festivals bring clowns and entertainers together from across the globe to compete, collaborate, and entertain audiences.

Events would include custard pie relays, unicycle races, juggling contests, culminating with spectacular pratfalls. Above all, this is a celebration of laughter and creativity, binding the world together through clowning.

Whether they love circus acts authentic to the layman or impromptu street acts or mere characters in film and television,the Clown Olympics is dedicated to the age-old art of spreading happiness through laughter.

Current Champion

David Larible is considered one of the best living clowns, having won multiple awards and accolades for his incredible circus performances.
