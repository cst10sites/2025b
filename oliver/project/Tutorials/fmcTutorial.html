FMC or fewest move competition is one of the most unique events. You get one hour to come up with the best solution you can. The video above is a tutorial for NISS (normal-inverse scramble switch) where you can switch from the normal scramble to the inverse scramble to find better cases..



These 2 videos above will help with being more efficient at building blocks, which is a technique often used in FMC.



You may recognize some of this from 3blind. Your own commutators can be useful in FMC for more efficiency but not required.



Skeletons and Insertions can be a very useful tool in FMC giving you some more options then building blocks and regular CFOP.



This is a more advanced tutorial for FMC. rNISS (reverse NISS) is used for visualization and identifying places to use insertion.
