Coyotes are clever, adaptable mammals that have learned to survive and even thrive in urban environments like Vancouver. These wild canines are often spotted trotting through parks, alleyways, and even quiet streets. If you spot a coyote, avoid feeding or approaching it.

Facts:

  • Scientific name: Canis latrans
  • Average size: 9–14 kg (20–30 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 10–14 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: Open grasslands, forests, deserts, urban and suburban areas
  • Food: Mainly small mammals and rodents like rabbits

Indigenous connection:

Coyotes are powerful characters in many traditional Indigneous stories. They are usually represented as clever and mischievous tricksters that are known for causing chaos while also teaching lessons to the other characters.
