Harbour seals are marine mammals often seen lounging on rocks or in the water along the Pacific coast. They are curious creatures that love to explore but they prefer to keep their distance from humans. Vancouver’s coastlines are a common place to spot them, most commonly they can be spotted around False Creek and the Fraser River.

Facts:

  • Scientific name: Phoca vitulina
  • Average size: 1.5 m, 55–150 kg
  • Lifespan: 25–30 years
  • Habitat: Coastal waters and rocky shores
  • Food: Fish and crustaceans
