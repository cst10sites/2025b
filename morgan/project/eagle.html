Bald eagles are a powerful bird of prey native to North America, most often found near lakes, rivers, and coastal areas. It is easily recognized by its white head and tail, contrasting with its dark brown body and wings. Known for its sharp eyesight and impressive wingspan, bald eagles hunt for all their food since they are carnivores and they often swoop down to snatch prey with its strong talons.

Facts:

  • Scientific name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus
  • Average size: Adult males are about 90 cm long, while females can reach up to 108 cm. Males have a wingspan of about 2 meters, and females can have a wingspan of up to 2.5 meters. 1
  • Lifespan: around 20-30 years old
  • Habitat: Areas with tall trees or cliffs for nesting and perching sometimes near large lakes, rivers, or coastal waters
  • Food: Fish, small mammals and small birds

Indigenous Connection:

In many Indigenous cultures across North America, eagles are deeply respected and spiritually significant. They are seen as messengers between the physical world and the spirit world because they fly so high and close to the Creator. Their feathers are sacred and often used in ceremonies, prayers, and regalia to represent honor, courage, and a strong connection to the Creator.
