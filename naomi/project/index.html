Female Hockey in Bc

Welcome to the World of Girls' Hockey in BC!

This website is dedicated to providing information about hockey opportunities in British Columbia for teen girls. Whether you're playing in the U15, U18 categories or looking to join a spring team, you’ll find the resources here.


There’s tons to be proud of when it comes to girls’ hockey in BC. The game has grown so much, and being part of it means you're part of something powerful. We’ve come a long way; from barely having teams to cheer for, to now filling rinks with strong, skilled, determined girls who love the game. That doesn’t mean it’s always been easy. Ice time isn’t always fair, teams can be far apart, and sometimes you feel like you have to work twice as hard to even get noticed. But those struggles have only made us stronger, and more passionate. The friendships built in ice rinks, the feeling of going bardownski, the pride of wearing your jersey; it all adds up to something special. Every time you show up, work hard, and have fun, your proof that female howckey is on the rise. Keep shwoing up. You’re part of the reason the future looks so bright.

Canadian Hall of Fame!

Hayley Wickenheiser started playing hockey as a kid on a backyard rink in Saskatchewan. She played on boys' teams growing up and made Team Canada at just 15. She went on to compete in five Olympics, winning four gold medals and one silver. Hayley also won seven World Championship golds and became Canada’s all-time leading scorer. She made history by playing on men’s pro teams in Europe and later became a doctor and a Toronto Maple Leafs executive. She’s in both the Hockey Hall of Fame and IIHF Hall of Fame—a true legend in women’s hockey.

Jenn Gardiner, from Surrey, BC, started playing hockey with local teams before starring with the Greater Vancouver Comets. She played college hockey at Ohio State, winning two NCAA championships and finishing with over 170 career points. In 2024, she was drafted by PWHL Montréal and had a strong rookie season, later signing with PWHL Vancouver. She also made Team Canada in 2025, scoring in the World Championship and Rivalry Series. She's known for her skill, clutch goals, and BC pride.

Natalie Spooner started playing hockey as a kid in Ontario and quickly rose through the ranks. She became Ohio State’s all-time leading goal scorer and won the Clarkson Cup with the Toronto Furies. She’s won Olympic gold with Team Canada in 2014 and 2022 and was named IIHF Female Player of the Year in 2024. Beyond playing, Natalie runs a hockey academy to help young girls grow in the sport.

Sara Nurse, from Hamilton, Ontario, started hockey young and played for the University of Wisconsin, where she helped win three WCHA championships. She joined Team Canada in 2015 and won Olympic silver in 2018 and gold in 2022, setting records for most points and assists at the 2022 Games. Off the ice, Sara is a leader for diversity and inclusion in hockey, helped create the PWHPA, and made history as the first woman on the NHL 23 video game cover. She’s an inspiring player and advocate for the sport’s future.

Chloe Primerano, from North Vancouver, BC, made history as the first female skater drafted into the WHL by the Vancouver Giants in 2022. She’s a top defender and set a record for most points by a defender at the U18 Women’s World Championship, winning MVP and Best Defender. Now at the University of Minnesota, she’s already making waves as a rookie star. Chloe’s quickly becoming a rising star in women’s hockey.
