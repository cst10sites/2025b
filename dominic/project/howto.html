Film Dialogue

Vary Your Shots: Don't just stick to alternating over-the-shoulder shots. Use a mix of: Wide Shots/Establishing Shots: To show the characters' relationship to their environment and each other, especially at the beginning or end of a conversation. Two-Shots: To emphasize shared moments, tension, or intimacy between two characters. Over-the-Shoulder (OTS) Shots: The workhorse for conversations, but ensure good depth of field (shallow for intimacy, deeper for context). The foreground shoulder should be slightly out of focus and not too dominant. Clean Singles/Close-Ups: For emotional impact, showing subtle reactions, or emphasizing key lines. These are where you see the "acting" in the eyes. Inserts/Cutaways: Shots of hands, objects, feet, or relevant background details. These break up visual monotony, provide context, or allow for smoother editing.
