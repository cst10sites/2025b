SCP-139

About

Item #: SCP-139

Object Class: Keter

Description: SCP-139 is a Bechstein Grand Piano, crafted colorfully. It was found in █████████, Budapest, before being tranwported to Vienna, Austria, and it dates from approximately the mid-19th century. SCP-139 appears to be a very ancient but durable piano, bearing scratch marks and seemingly beat down. However, it does not contain any defects and still functions as a normal piano (aside from its anomalous properties).

Anomalous Properties

Figure A: Paused frame taken from video footage of Runthrough I

Upon seeing SCP-139, one will immediately shift their focus onto the piano and will begin advancing slowly towards the piano unknowingly. If no one is performing, then they will feel a need to sit on the bench. When one sits down on the piano bench, they will feel an urge to begin playing the piano, even if they do not have any experience with playing the piano beforehand. After up to 2 minutes, this urge becomes irresistable, to a point where they feel obligated to play and will suffer from a stroke or other forms of harm should they continue resisting.

The SCP-139 Effect

The SCP-139 effect is a memetic effect that causes one to immediately excel at their piano playing. When one begins playing on SCP-139, they automatically begin playing one of Liszt's pieces, even if they did not intend to. Their performance is also immaculate, nearly without any errors, and they play with a lot of musicality and expression. During the performance, the player will think of many things relating to the context of the piece, and these imaginations will be projected. This can also cause reality bending; the performer's imagination while playing will be cast and can also modify the scene, and this can affect both the player and the listeners/observers. This can be dangerous, as should the context of the piece be violent, all individuals near SCP-139 are subjected to the chaos.

Molto Vivace (Rockets), S. 139/2 Played with an unrivalled amount of energy by D-605439, an intermediate level pianist. The dynamic contrasts were shown very clearly, and the pianist was unfazed by the immense difficulties, including the giant jumps, fast alternating chords, and notey runs. As soon as the piece started, a bomb appeared. Despite attempts of defusing it, it exploded right after the introduction of the piece, killing 3 guards and wounding 7. As the music continued, SCP-139 continued making explosions, and created an immense burst at the end, killing the performer (D-605439), all guards in the testing room, and others in adjacent rooms. This caused a lockdown to be initiated, and SCP-139 was forbidden to be touched again for a year.
Paysage, S. 139/3 Performed with great care, with smooth and articulated phrases. Dynamics contrasts effectively boosted the power of the climax, and the decreasing ending was very effectively conveyed. All individuals near SCP-139 felt overwhelmed with joy and calmness, pleasantly surprising the new guards after the massacre that was of the previous piece. The climax gave a deep sense of peacefulness, and the build-ups and ending felt immensely musically riveting. Well. This one went better than the last one. That should signify the end of all the unhinged, disastrous, and tumultuous music, no? -Dr. Y
Mazeppa, S. 139/4 The virtuosity was reportedly unmatched. The opening cadenza was played with no hesitation, and powerfully transitioned into the relentless main theme. The middle section was played tenderly, but the recapitulation took the violence of the opening to another level. Finally, the heroic coda, encapsulating all the previously shown power of the pieces, bought the piece to its well-deserved grandiose end. As soon as the opening chords struck, the location changed to that of a wild forest. Immediately, the player (D-230931) lost his clothes and got strapped to a wild horse, with SCP-139 instead playing the piece by itself (?). The horse began galloping savagely across the forest while the guards tried to stop it and chase it down with limited success. They eventually decided to try and use a safeguard protocol to revert the scene back to the original Foundation site (instead of the newly projected forest) but were seemingly unsuccessful. However, unknowingly to them, this did revert the actual terrain back to that of the site. As the piece ended, the projected scenery disappeared and unveiled the site, now nearly completely demolished by the force of the horse rampaging across it. ...And the award for the worst prediction of the year goes to Dr. Y. -Dr. B

As soon as they arrived, they went directly to ██████████, the location where SCP-139 was transported to. There, they witnessed a tall, fit man with longer hair and formal dresses performing a 30-minutes long piece. As soon as the men approached him, he thanked them for listening to his performance before vanishing into thin air.
